Foundation


My foundation was shaped through several reinforcing tracks.

An engineering education trained me to think in systems — inputs, dependencies, failure modes, and feedback loops. An MBA reframed that same instinct through the language of markets, customers, and capital. Stanford’s SCPM and PMP added the operational discipline required to turn ideas into delivered outcomes at scale.

Two decades of commercial leadership across telecom, enterprise technology, and Gov-Tech compounded on top of that foundation — owning P&Ls, building channels, commercializing products, and scaling enablement systems across three continents. Each role added another layer: how to design GTM motions, govern commercialization end-to-end, build operating systems that turn strategy into predictable revenue execution, and more recently how to embed AI into commercial workflows without losing the human judgment that makes them effective.

That combination is what I bring to the work today — engineering rigor, commercial fluency, and operational discipline shaped through large-scale execution environments and continuous adaptation. It is what allows me to move from boardroom strategy to operating-model design in the same conversation, and from commercial vision to hands-on execution without losing either.
