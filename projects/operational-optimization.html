Sales Enablement

Ooredoo Qatar's SMB sales force spans 100+ indirect channel partners, direct field reps, and retail staff — each expected to sell an increasingly complex product portfolio in a highly competitive, saturated market.

The sales team was growing in headcount but not in performance. New reps took up to four months to become productive, top talent was indistinguishable from average performers until months into the role, and the team lacked the tools, content, and in-store infrastructure to engage customers effectively. 1

B2B Sales Academy

New sales representatives were taking up to four months to become productive — an unsustainable cost of underperformance with no reliable mechanism to identify high-potential talent early in their tenure.

Conceived and built Ooredoo's first structured B2B Sales Academy — a first-of-its-kind programme in the organization with a formal curriculum, product certification tracks, and performance benchmarks embedded from day one. The curriculum covered product knowledge, consultative selling techniques, CRM and SFA tool proficiency, competitive positioning, and objection handling.

The certification model meant reps completed structured product modules and were assessed before being cleared to sell — enabling them to engage customers and close deals on the same day training was completed. This compressed the ramp-up period from 4 months to just 1.

Equally valuable was the early performance signal the Academy created. The structured assessments and early selling activity made it possible to identify star performers within the first few weeks on the job — enabling faster promotion decisions, targeted coaching for developing reps, and smarter resource allocation across the team. Average individual rep performance improved by 15%, and the division sustained 5% YoY revenue growth in a saturated, highly competitive market.

2

Territory Design & Geographic Zoning

The field sales force operated without defined territories — reps overlapped on the same accounts, large parts of the metro area went unvisited, and there was no accountability for geographic coverage. Market surveys confirmed only 62% of the addressable area was being actively served.

Designed and implemented a comprehensive geographic zoning programme, dividing the metropolitan area into 65 discrete sales zones, each assigned to a dedicated agent with full ownership and accountability for that territory. Integrated Esri ArcGIS with the CRM to calculate zone size, account density, and revenue potential — enabling data-driven boundary decisions that ensured equitable workload distribution while eliminating coverage gaps and agent overlap.

Zones were configured end-to-end in the CRM and SFA platform, so all outbound calling lists and inbound leads were automatically routed to the agent responsible for that zone — creating a seamless link between data-driven prospecting and field follow-up. Field agents were equipped with a mobile app enabling real-time updates back into the CRM from the field — capturing new customer contacts, active competitor products, account status changes, and market intelligence as it happened. This turned every field visit into a live data collection event, keeping the CRM continuously enriched and current.

Within three months, confirmed market coverage improved from 62% to 89%, verified by independent customer survey — a 27-point gain that directly expanded the addressable pipeline and contributed to the division's sustained revenue growth.

3

Centralized Sales Content Management

Sales materials were scattered across personal folders and shared drives — teams regularly worked from outdated pricing, stale presentations, and unofficial product specs, undermining both credibility and conversion.

Sourced and deployed a centralized Content Management platform as the single source of truth for all sales collateral — customer presentations, product specs, pricing sheets, promotions, training materials, and order forms. Drove adoption across the full sales team through structured onboarding sessions. Established a governance process with Base Management and Product Managers to ensure content was continuously reviewed and current. Equipped every field representative with a connected tablet, giving them real-time access to collateral, customer data, and order processing in the field.

4

In-Store & Field Engagement Enablement

Sales staff lacked effective tools to engage walk-in customers in retail locations, resulting in missed upsell opportunities, inconsistent product conversations, and a flat in-store experience.

Designed an interactive product and services catalog tailored for both shop and field environments. Deployed it on interactive screens across all retail locations for self-serve customer browsing, and equipped field reps with the same catalog for guided, consistent customer conversations. Integrated promotional messaging into the queue management system so customers received relevant content while waiting — turning passive wait time into an active sales touchpoint.

5

Order Digitization & Cycle Time Reduction

Non-standardized, incomplete paper order forms were causing downstream errors, fulfillment delays, and customer friction — with the order-to-cash cycle routinely stretching to 48 hours.

Partnered with Product, Operations, and Marketing UX/UI teams to redesign and standardize all order forms into clean, fillable PDF documents. Centralized them in the sales repository for online completion, download, or direct sharing with customers. Established a digital signing workflow enabling authorized customer signatories to execute orders remotely — compressing the order-to-cash cycle from 48 hours to under one hour.

→
Together, these five initiatives transformed the sales force from reactive and unstructured to capable, structured, and measurably productive. By investing in people first — through the Academy — defining their territory, equipping them with the right tools and content, and removing friction from the order process, the team achieved sustained 5% YoY growth in one of the most competitive telecom markets in the region.
